Cl2 and Br2, which one is more reactive, which one is more selectivetive?


Introduction
This question is very inrteresting and important. it is relate to the key point of the radicals.
Body
Reactivity toward alkanes via radical halogenation
F2 > Cl2 > Br2 > I2 because F• > Cl• > Br• > I•
Why: Chlorine is more reactive the bromine because chlorine radical
is less stable then bromine radical.
Electrophilicity (Reactivity in SN2, SN1, E2, E1 Reactions)
X-I > X-Br >X-Cl
As carbon-halogen bond stability increases, the reactivity decreases.
The selectivity is due to the energy levels needed to break the bonds. The more stable the radical, the easier it is to break the bond. Because bromine has less energy than chlorine, it is more selective.
Bromine is much more selective Chlorine is much less selective

Summary

In summary, Bromine is much more selective Chlorine is much more reactive.
